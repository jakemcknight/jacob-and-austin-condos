## Data Dive

The downtown real estate market is experiencing reduced transaction volume compared to pre-pandemic levels. Historical averages show 233 transactions annually before 2020, but 2024's pace suggests only 166 transactions by year-end.

**Downtown Market Biweekly Snapshot**

| Metric | Current | Previous | Change |
|--------|---------|----------|--------|
| Total Active Listings | 201 | 206 | -2% |
| New Listings | 14 | 16 | -13% |
| Pending | 15 | 7 | +53% |
| Closed | 5 | 4 | +20% |

New supply decreased while demand increased, continuing momentum from the previous update.

## Stay in the Loop

**Interview with Brett Rhode** -- An [interview with Brett Rhode](https://www.bizjournals.com/austin/news/2024/08/19/journal-profile-brett-rhode-architect-austin.html), architect at Rhode Partners who designed The Independent, The Linden, and The Loren.

**Google's Vacant Sail Tower** -- Google's [billion-dollar Texas investment](https://www.bizjournals.com/austin/news/2024/08/15/google-billion-investment-texas.html) focuses primarily on data centers. Despite employing 2,000 in Austin, they have not occupied the "Sail Tower" downtown office building. Market-rate leasing would cost approximately $53.2 million annually.

**Bat Watching Season** -- Labor Day weekend offers prime [bat viewing season](https://www.austintexas.org/things-to-do/outdoors/bat-watching/) at the Congress Avenue Bridge. Best times occur around sunset, with shows running 7:30-9:45 p.m. and taking up to 45 minutes for all bats to exit.
